Why More Than One Type

Having a diverse range of tree species in your yard or on a larger piece of acreage is essential for fostering a healthy and resilient ecosystem. Different tree species provide a variety of benefits, such as improving soil health, supporting biodiversity, and creating habitats for various wildlife. For example, some trees may attract pollinators, while others can serve as shelter for birds and small animals. Additionally, a mix of species can help prevent the spread of diseases and pests that often target specific types of trees. Furthermore, diverse trees contribute to the aesthetic appeal of a property, offering a range of colors, shapes, and textures throughout the seasons. In larger spaces, tree diversity also ensures long-term sustainability, as different species respond differently to environmental changes, such as drought or flooding, thereby safeguarding the area against ecological challenges. This variety enhances not only the health of the land but also its ability to thrive for generations to come.
